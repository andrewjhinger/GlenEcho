Glen Echo Historic District

Architecture Gallery

Craftsman Style
411 Arcadia Ave (Gregg House, 1911–12)
Bungalow with signature wide porch, exposed rafters, and stone foundation. Typical of the Arts & Crafts movement in Glen Echo.
Shingle Style
2620–2622 Summit St (Lehman House, 1911–12)
Asymmetrical 2½-story double dwelling, covered in wood shingles, reflecting the flowing, informal style popular in the early 1900s.
Tudor Revival
2570 Summit (Evans House, 1911–12)
Classic Tudor with steep gables, decorative half-timbering, and multi-pane windows. Emphasizes storybook charm and craftsmanship.
Colonial Revival
2539 N. Fourth Street (Downs House, 1916)
Blonde brick with symmetrical facade, Palladian window, and classical details. A reflection of early 20th-century American tradition.
Gable End House
2561 Glen Echo Drive
Frame or brick homes with the gable facing the street, often featuring porches and Craftsman or Classical ornamentation.
Dormer-Front Bungalow
2612 Summit Street
Bungalows with broad front dormers, inset porches, and relaxed living spaces. Distinctively American.
American Four-Square
2676 Summit Street
Boxy, practical two-story homes with a central dormer and full-width porch, echoing the efficiency of early suburbia.
Side-Gabled House
2564 Glen Echo Dr.
Homes with gable roofs running parallel to the street, featuring front porches and diverse detailing.
Gabled Ell Variation
2584 Indianola Avenue
L-shaped homes with intersecting roofs, wraparound porches, and intricate shingle work.
